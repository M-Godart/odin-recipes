Stacked Tomato and Burrata

Description

All you need to know about this tomato and Burrata salad is that burrata means “buttered.” Comparing mozzarella and Burrata is slightly unfair, as Burrata is significantly richer and creamier. It's not like comparing apples and oranges — it's more like apples and supermodels. By John Mitzewich

Ingredients

  • 1 vine-ripened tomato, cored and cut into 1/2 slices
  • flaked sea salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup Burrata cheese, or more to taste
  • 1 tablespoon torn fresh basil leaves, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Place a tomato slice onto a plate and season with sea salt and black pepper.
  2. Spread Burrata cheese over tomato slice, top with torn basil leaves, and drizzle with olive oil.
  3. Repeat the layering process with remaining ingredients, finishing with a tomato slice.
